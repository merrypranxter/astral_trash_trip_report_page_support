# tr-0002 — The Unstuffing (Shower Scrub Ritual)

## Raw Narrative (as dictated)

Recurring end-of-trip pattern on mushrooms, at home, alone. She ends up in the shower —
exfoliating scrubby gloves, Dr. Bronner's peppermint soap, scrubbing the shit out of
herself. The running water and closed door cut off the bedroom music; nothing comes in.
It's just her and the thoughts.

Underneath the thoughts, she notices a background process that's been running the whole
time: **"I want out. I want out. I want out."** Not focused, not forced — a loop running
behind everything. And underneath that: *I want this off of me.*

Then the inquiry: what is "this"? Not the skin — taking skin off would hurt, would leave
a bloody pulp. Not the body. **The STUFF. The dense matter.** She wants the dense
matter off of her.

So the shower became a ritual: scrubbing the dense matter off her soul — or acting like
she is, if she could. Frequency: roughly 60% of the time, at the end of trips, when home
alone.

## The Ritual Sequence

1. **Isolation** — water noise + closed door seal the chamber; no music, no input
2. **Detection** — the background loop surfaces into awareness ("I want out")
3. **Inquiry** — "what do I want off of me?" — skin rejected via the bloody-pulp test
4. **Identification** — it's the STUFF; the dense matter; the stuffness itself
5. **Operation** — vigorous scrubbing as symbolic/energetic removal; the soul gets exfoliated
6. **Exit** — the removed goes down the drain; one-way only
